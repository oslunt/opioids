The Opioid Epidemic:

How did we get here?



  • 1999

    Wave 1

    Opioid prescription rates increased significant in the 1990s, correlating with marked prescription overdose deaths.


  • 2010

    Wave 2

    Deaths involving use of Heroin, an illicit and more powerful opioid drug, begin to rise sharply in 2010.


  • 2013

    Wave 3

    Unprecendented increases of overdose deaths involving synthetic opioids are recorded. Many of these deaths are traced back to illicitly manufactured Fentanyl and Tramadol.


  • 2021

    Current Crisis

    Between 1999 and 2019, nearly 500,000 people died from an opioid overdose, including both prescription and illicit opioids. Today, 136 people die every day from an opioid overose.

How should I talk about opioids?

Avoid words such as "addict" and "abuse."
Rather, say "Someone struggling with Substance Use Disorder," "Substance misuse," and "Struggling with addiction."
